Transform the json into a txt with the qualified test names

cat __results.json | jq -r 'to_entries[] | .key as $class | .value | keys[] | "\($class).\(.)"' > test_names.txt

Remove all #

sed -i 's/#/./g' test_names.txt
awk '!a[$0]++' test_names.txt > test_names_uniq.txt

Remove malformed results

grep "\." test_names_uniq.txt > test_names_filtered.csv

Count tests in common between file and dataset:

All

awk -F ',' 'NR==FNR{a[$1]; next} $4 in a' test_names_filtered.csv /home/alex/Desktop/dataset/Original/Tests.csv | wc -l

OD

awk -F ',' 'NR==FNR{a[$1]; next} $4 in a' test_names_filtered.csv /home/alex/Desktop/dataset/Original/Tests_OD_only.csv | wc -l

NOD

awk -F ',' 'NR==FNR{a[$1]; next} $4 in a' test_names_filtered.csv /home/alex/Desktop/dataset/Original/Tests_NOD_only.csv | wc -l
